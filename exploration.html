Not sure what career direction is right for you? That is completely normal. UMSI graduates pursue a wide range of roles across industries including technology, healthcare, finance, government, nonprofit, and more.

Use these resources to explore your options, reflect on your interests and strengths, and learn about the many career paths available to School of Information students.

Steps to Explore Your Career

  1. Reflect on Your Interests: What topics, projects, or coursework excite you the most? Write down the skills you enjoy using.
  2. Research Roles and Industries: Browse job postings, read career profiles, and look at where UMSI alumni have landed.
  3. Talk to Professionals: Conduct informational interviews to learn firsthand what different roles are really like.
  4. Try Things Out: Pursue internships, volunteer roles, or student projects to gain hands-on experience in areas that interest you.
  5. Meet with a Career Coach: A CDO coach can help you clarify your goals and develop a plan.

Exploration FAQ

  • What if I do not know what career I want?
    That is okay. Career exploration is a process. Start by reflecting on what you enjoy and talking to people in different roles.
  • Can I change directions later?
    Yes. Many professionals change career paths throughout their lives. The skills you build at UMSI are transferable across many industries.
  • How do internships help with exploration?
    Internships let you experience a role and industry firsthand, helping you understand what you enjoy and what you want to avoid.
  • Where can I see what alumni are doing?
    LinkedIn is a great resource. Search for UMSI alumni and filter by industry, company, or role to see the range of career paths.
