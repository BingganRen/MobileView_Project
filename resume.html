Your resume is often your first impression with an employer. Whether you are starting from scratch or polishing an existing document, these resources will help you highlight your unique strengths, skills, and experiences.

UMSI students are in high demand, and a clear, well-organized resume will help you stand out. Need feedback? You can always meet with a CDO Career Coach to review your materials.

Five Steps to a Strong Resume

  1. Download a UMSI-Approved Template: Start with a format that works so you can focus on content, not design.
  2. Gather Your Experiences: List jobs, internships, volunteer roles, coursework, and projects most relevant to your goals.
  3. Write Impactful Bullets: Use action verbs and highlight your skills, results, and unique contributions.
  4. Tailor for Each Opportunity: Customize your resume for the specific requirements and keywords of each role.
  5. Review and Edit: Proofread carefully, check formatting, and ask for feedback from peers or a career coach.

Resume FAQ

  • How long should my resume be?
    For most UMSI students and new graduates, one page is best. More experienced professionals may use two pages.
  • Do I need a different resume for each application?
    Yes. Tailoring your resume to each opportunity's requirements and keywords will strengthen your application.
  • Can I include coursework or student projects?
    Absolutely. UMSI coursework and projects are valued by employers, especially when they demonstrate relevant skills and impact.
  • How often should I update my resume?
    Update every semester, after major projects, or whenever you gain a new role or responsibility.
